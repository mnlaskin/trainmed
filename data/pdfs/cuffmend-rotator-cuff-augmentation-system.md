CuffMend‚ĄĘ Rotator Cuff 
Augmentation System
Providing an efficient, simple approach to augmenting partial- and full-
thickness rotator cuff tears, the CuffMend system incorporates either 
ArthroFlex¬ģ dermal allograft for supplemental strength, 1,2 or autograft 
biceps. Insert the graft using the graft spreader and then securely 
fixate the graft using FiberStitch RC implants, for medial fixation 
and Knotless PushLock ¬ģ anchors for lateral bony fixation. Scientific 
literature has demonstrated that rotator cuff augmentation with 
acellular dermal graft can improve healing and reduce retear rates. 2 
ArthroFLEX¬ģ Dermal Allograft
ArthroFlex dermal allograft is a biohospitable acellular 
dermal allograft intended for supplemental support and 
covering for soft-tissue repair. 1,3,4 
LifeNet Health‚Äôs patented and validated Matracell ¬ģ 
decellularization process renders the ArthroFlex 
dermal allograft acellular without compromising its 
biomechanical or biochemical properties. 3,4 Matracell 
technology removes donor DNA from the dermal matrix, 
while retaining its native growth factors, collagen, and 
elastin. 3,5 Matracell technology is validated to remove 
‚Č•97% of donor DNA and cellular content. 3
ArthroFlex dermal allograft is treated with Preservon ¬ģ, a 
proprietary and patented preservation technology that 
allows the graft to be fully hydrated at room temperature. 3

Lateral Portal
Use a spinal needle to create a lateral working portal for 
placement of a 10 mm PassPort Button‚ĄĘ cannula. It is very 
important to make the portal as parallel as possible to 
the surface of the rotator cuff, resulting in a lateral portal 
location that will be more inferior compared to a standard 
lateral working portal. 
Optional Inferior Anterolateral Portal
An auxiliary inferior anterolateral portal may also 
be created to introduce the graft spreader into the 
subacromial space.
