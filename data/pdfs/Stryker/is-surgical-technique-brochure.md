# InSpace Balloon Implant Surgical Technique Guide

Surgical 
technique guide
InSpace balloon implant
A minimally-invasive biodegradable, subacromial spacer for 
arthroscopic treatment of massive, irreparable rotator cuff tears

Elevating motion 
Rotator cuff tears (RCTs) are amongst the most frequent orthopedic conditions 
in adults and are one of the most common sources of pain and dysfunction in the 
shoulder. The incidence of rotator cuff tears may increase in frequency with age 
and is often associated with degeneration of the tendons.
The management of massive irreparable rotator cuff tears is challenging and 
associated with high failure rates1. There is a need within the shoulder continuum 
of care for alternative surgical treatment options to meet the needs of patients. 
The InSpace balloon implant provides an option for surgeons to use a streamlined 
surgical procedure. The implant is designed to restore the subacromial space 
without requiring sutures or fixation devices and has been demonstrated to 
improve shoulder motion and function2. 
The InSpace implant has been commercially available outside the United States 
since receiving the CE Mark in 2010 and has recently received marketing clearance 
in the United States. With over 29,000 implantations worldwide, the extensive 
global clinical experience with the InSpace implant demonstrates good outcomes, 
low incidence of device-related complications, and ease of use3. 
The InSpace subacromial tissue spacer system is indicated for the treatment of 
patients with massive, irreparable full-thickness torn rotator cuff tendons due 
to trauma or degradation with mild to moderate gleno-humeral osteoarthritis in 
patients greater than or equal to 65 years of age whose clinical conditions would 
benefit from treatment with a shorter surgical time compared to partial rotator 
cuff repair.

Directions for use
The InSpace implant comes in 3 sizes:
Small Medium Large 
To select the appropriate implant size:
• Through a standard lateral portal measure ~1cm medial to the glenoid 
rim to the lateral boarder of the greater tuberosity
• Based on measurement, choose the appropriate InSpace size in 
accordance with the guidelines in Table 1
• Introduce the InSpace deployer through a standard lateral portal. Care 
should be taken to ensure correct positioning of the InSpace implant 
~1cm medial to the glenoid rim to the lateral border 
of the greater tuberosity. 
 Note: Widen incision on lateral portal to accommodate the diameter 
of the protective sheath, which is approximately 9mm
 Note: The mark on the protective sheath should be over the lateral 
border of the greater tuberosity
Perform a standard subacromial 
arthroscopy. As necessitated 
by individual shoulder 
condition, perform arthroscopic 
debridement of the subacromial 
space, which may include 
removal of the bursa or removal 
of osteophytes to allow distance 
measurements and proper 
device placement. 
Fill the syringe with sterile saline to the recommended volume based on 
the measured distance in Table 1.
• Remove air bubbles from the syringe (and extension tube with/without 
3-way valve) prior to connecting to the InSpace delivery system
Once the InSpace delivery system is positioned, attach the syringe 
(and extension tube with/without 3-way valve) to the InSpace 
delivery system.
Inflate the implant to maximum volume (see Table 1). 
Carefully draw back the syringe plunger to deflate the implant 
slightly, leaving only the recommended final volume of sterile 
saline inside the implant (see Table 1).
Note: Assistance may be required to hold the InSpace delivery 
system in the correct position as the implant is inflated
Note: You will feel some resistance as you depress the syringe 
to fill the balloon, especially if a long length or a small diameter 
extension tubing is used
Safety lock button
Once the recommended final volume is reached, keep the delivery 
system stable, slide the red safety lock button forward.
Perform gentle maneuvers of the patient’s arm to verify 
correct positioning and stability of the implant, prior to sealing 
and detachment.
Once correct positioning is confirmed, release the red backstopper and 
pull back the protective sheath which will expose the implant. 
Note: Once the red backstopper is released and you have retracted 
the protective sheath you are committed to the positioning of the 
implant (any movement would risk possible breakage or puncture of 
the implant)
Release lever
Rotate the green release lever clockwise. 
This will seal the implant and leave it in situ.
Once the implant is sealed, withdraw the delivery system 
and verify again that the implant is stable, in its proper 
location and does not interfere with passive maneuvers 
of the shoulder’s range of motion.
Patient preparation
Patients should receive prophylactic broad-spectrum 
antibiotics per local/institutional guidelines for orthopedic 
implants prior to InSpace implantation.
Safety lock button
During insertion, release and filling of the balloon, 
the red safety lock button should be facing the 
underside of the acromion.

Positioning and set-up
A standard arrangement for arthroscopic rotator cuff repair arthroscopy 
is used. Either beach chair or the lateral decubitus position is appropriate.
