# Suture Kit Step-by-Step Guide Brochure (XBraid TT)

Step 1 
Utilizing four NiceLoop Sutures, begin the procedure by passing the 
sutures through the tendon-bone junction of the infraspinatus and 
teres minor tendons on the greater tuberosity.
Step 3
Pass all four sutures through the medial suture groove when 
implanting the stem. Take two of the horizontal NiceLoop sutures 
and tie down to reduce the greater tuberosity.
Note: The CCS-1 needle is designed to be removed from the 
NiceLoop suture by hand.
Step 5
Tuberosities are secured and reduced to the stem once sutures are 
tied off.
Note: The CCS-1 needle is designed to be removed from the 
NiceLoop suture by hand.
Step 2
Prior to inserting final implant, drill in diaphysis to place two 
vertical tension NiceLoop sutures as demonstrated above.
Step 4
The remaining two NiceLoop sutures will pass through the 
subscapularis tendon (inferior and superior portions) to reduce the 
lesser tuberosity.
Step 6
For the vertical NiceLoop sutures, pass the anterior suture 
through the subscapularis and the posterior suture through the 
infraspinatus.
