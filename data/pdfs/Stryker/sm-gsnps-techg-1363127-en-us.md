AlphaVent to Omega rotator cuff repair
surgical procedure
Through the lateral portal, align the AlphaVent awl 
perpendicular to the bone at the articular margin. 
Mallet the awl down to the laser line and remove. 
Repeat with a second AlphaVent anchor. 
Bring the AlphaVent anchor in through the lateral 
portal and place it perpendicular over the pilot hole. 
Rotate clockwise until the anchor’s proximal surface is 
flush with the bone. The dual thread technology offers 
tactile feedback during cortical thread engagement. 
Release the sutures from the cleat. Pull straight back 
on the inserter handle to remove. 
Note: AlphaVent PN 3910947021 is utilized for this 
specific technique. Additional offerings are listed at 
the end of this guide. 
To pass the AlphaVent sutures through the rotator cuff 
tissue, bring two tails of the 1.4mm XBraid TT from 
the anterior anchor through the lateral portal. 
Load the end of the passing loop into the Champion 
suture passer. Through the loop, load the two 
suture tails.

Advance the passing loop through the cuff and pull the 
two suture tails through with the loop. 
Bring two tails of the 1.4mm XBraid TT from the 
anterior anchor through the lateral portal. Tie them 
down to achieve additional medial fixation. Repeat for 
the posterior anchor. 
Repeat this process for the remaining suture tails. 
Retrieve an untied suture tail from each anchor and 
a tied suture tail from each anchor through the lateral 
portal. 
If needed, the passing loop may be used 
prophylactically to complete a dog ear reduction.

Load these sutures into the Omega eyelet using the 
pull tab.
With the distal tip of the screwdriver, follow the 
guiding suture path down to the aperture of the hole. 
Place the tip of the screw over the pilot hole. 
Position the tip of the eyelet on the greater tuberosity. 
Mallet the inserter until the distal edge of the laser line 
is flush with the bone. Pull straight back to remove the 
eyelet inserter, releasing the eyelet in the bone.
Note: The eyelet inserter is self-punching. However, if 
the surgeon determines a pilot hole is needed, 
instrumentation, including awls, drills, and taps, is 
available. See IFU P44444 for pilot hole creation 
instructions. 
 
While maintaining slight downward pressure on the 
screwdriver, pull tension on the suture limbs one at 
a time. 
Depending on the quality of bone, when pulling 
tension, you may feel the eyelet reposition under the 
cortex. The eyelet’s proprietary design leverages bone 
density to guide its repositioning so that the eyelet may 
fixate in bone.1
